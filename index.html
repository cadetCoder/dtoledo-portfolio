Learning Journeys

Introduction to Data & Database Modelling

Week 1 & 2

During my data & database management course, I gained an understanding of Entity Relationship (ER) modelling in SQL, which is a powerful tool for creating effective and well-organised databases.

ER modelling involves visually representing entities, attributes, and relationships between them.

This technique provides a clearer understanding of complex data structures.

I learned how to apply ER modelling in SQL to design a database schema, and also identify potential data anomalies and inconsistencies that may impact data integrity.

Furthermore, the course equipped me with the skills to use SQL to query and manipulate data based on my ER model.

As a result, I can extract valuable insights and make data-driven decisions. In summary, this course provided me with a strong understanding of ER modelling using SQL, which is an important skill for the future job.

Database Modelling

Week 3

In week 3 in DBMS subject, we were introduced to seveval aspects of SQL, including constraints, functions and groups. I found this subject to be a fascinating one, and I want to gain this skill to benefit me in my future career.

SQL constraints are rules that are applied to a database to ensure that data is entered in a specific format, and they help to maintain the integrity of the database. I have learned about the different types of SQL contstraints, such as primary keys, foreign keys, and unique constraints, and how to use them effectively.

Week 4
